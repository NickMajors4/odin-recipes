Brown Stew Chicken

Description

Jamaican Brown Stew Chicken is a savory Caribbean classic where chicken is marinated in spices, browned for rich flavor, and simmered in a hearty sauce of onions, peppers, tomatoes, and herbs. The browning process gives the dish its deep, smoky taste while Scotch bonnet peppers add a gentle heat. Served with rice and peas or white rice, it’s a comforting and authentic taste of Jamaica.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 2 tbsp browning sauce
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 sprigs thyme
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  1. Season chicken with browning sauce, allspice, salt, pepper, and soy sauce. Marinate for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Brown chicken pieces on all sides, then remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, sauté onion, bell pepper, garlic, and Scotch bonnet until softened.
  4. Add tomatoes and thyme, cooking until tomatoes break down into a thick base.
  5. Return chicken to the pan, add chicken broth, and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30–40 minutes until chicken is tender and sauce thickens.
  7. Adjust seasoning as needed, then serve with rice and peas or white rice.
