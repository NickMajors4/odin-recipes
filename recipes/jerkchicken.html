Jerk Chicken

Description

Jerk chicken is a flavorful Jamaican dish known for its bold, smoky, and spicy taste. It’s traditionally made by marinating chicken in a mixture of aromatic spices, fresh herbs, Scotch bonnet peppers, and allspice (pimento), which is the signature ingredient of jerk seasoning. The chicken is then slow-cooked over pimento wood or grilled, allowing the marinade to deeply penetrate the meat while creating a deliciously charred, crispy exterior. This cooking method infuses the chicken with layers of heat, sweetness, and smokiness that are both distinctive and irresistible.Beyond its taste, jerk chicken carries a rich cultural heritage that traces back to the Maroons of Jamaica, who developed this cooking technique as a way to preserve and flavor meat. Today, it is a staple of Jamaican cuisine and is often served with classic sides like rice and peas, fried plantains, or festival bread. Whether prepared at a roadside stand or in a home kitchen, jerk chicken is celebrated worldwide as a vibrant representation of Jamaica’s culinary traditions and its bold, island flavors.

Ingredients

  • 3 lb of chicken of your choice
  • 3 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 4 green onion
  • 1 white onion
  • fresh thyme
  • 2 tbsp pimento seeds(Allspice)
  • 2 garlic bulb
  • 1 tbsp ginger
  • 1 tbsp cloves
  • 1 tbsp nutmeg
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp browning
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  1. First clean your chicken with water and lemon juice, as your cleaning the chicken put the ingredients above in a blender and blend until smooth.
  2. Once blended pour the marinade over the chicken and let it marinate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight in the fridge.
  3. After it's marinated you can either grill or put in the oven
  4. This recipe though is for the oven
  5. Set your oven to 400 degrees Farenheit
  6. Depending the type of chicken you chose it can take anywhere from 30 minutes to an hour to cook
  7. If cooking traditionally with leg quarters Cook to an internal temperature for optimal tenderness
  8. When the chicken is about done, turn on broil and leave in for 3-5 minutes to get a nice char
  9. Once done, let it rest for 5-10 minutes before serving
