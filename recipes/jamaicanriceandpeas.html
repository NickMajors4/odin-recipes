Rice and Peas

Rice and Peas is a classic Jamaican side dish made with fluffy rice cooked in creamy coconut milk, seasoned with thyme, scallions, and allspice, and studded with tender red kidney beans (the “peas” in the name). This fragrant dish is a staple in Caribbean cooking and is often paired with stews, curries, and jerk dishes. The combination of coconut richness and aromatic spices makes it a perfect complement to bold-flavored mains like brown stew chicken or jerk pork.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice (rinsed)
  • 1 cup canned red kidney beans (drained)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 2 cups water or chicken broth
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 scallions, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1 tsp allspice>
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Rinse rice until water runs clear and set aside.
  2. In a pot, combine coconut milk, water or broth, kidney beans, thyme, scallions, garlic, allspice, Scotch bonnet (if using), and salt. Bring to a boil.
  3. Add rice, stir once, and reduce heat to low.
  4. Cover and simmer for 18–20 minutes, or until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  5. Remove thyme sprigs and Scotch bonnet before serving.
  6. Fluff rice with a fork and serve hot alongside your favorite Jamaican main dish.
